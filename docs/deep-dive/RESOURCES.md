# Further Study & Resources

For those interested in the deep mathematics and physics behind this project, the following resources are highly recommended.

## 1. General Relativity (Concept & Intuition)
*   **[PBS Space-Time (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/c/pbsspacetime)**: The "General Relativity" playlist is the best visual introduction to curvature and geodesics.
*   **[Khan Academy: General Relativity](https://www.khanacademy.org)**: Intuitive breakdown of the Equivalence Principle.

## 2. Advanced Spacetime Mathematics
*   **[Schwarzschild Metric (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schwarzschild_metric)**: The exact solution for static, non-rotating bodies.
*   **[Kerr Metric (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerr_metric)**: The solution for rotating black holes and frame-dragging.
*   **[Misner, Thorne, and Wheeler (MTW) - "Gravitation"]**: Known as the "Big Black Book" of GR. It is the definitive graduate-level textbook on the subject.

## 3. Numerical Physics & Integration
*   **[Runge-Kutta Methods (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Runge%E2%80%93Kutta_methods)**: Understanding why RK4 is the "gold standard" for planetary simulation.
*   **[Solving ODEs in Python (SciPy)](https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/generated/scipy.integrate.solve_ivp.html)**: Documentation for the solvers that inspired the backend's integration logic.

## 4. Software Stack Documentation
*   **[React-Three-Fiber](https://docs.pmnd.rs/react-three-fiber/getting-started/introduction)**: How to bring 3D graphics into the React ecosystem.
*   **[FastAPI Documentation](https://fastapi.tiangolo.com/)**: High-performance Python backend framework.
*   **[Zustand State Management](https://github.com/pmndrs/zustand)**: Lightweight state management for high-frequency data.

## Technical Papers of Interest
*   **[Lense, J.; Thirring, H. (1918)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lense%E2%80%93Thirring_precession)**: Original paper on the "Frame Dragging" effect simulated in the Kerr scenario of this app.
*   **[Einstein, A. (1915)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity)**: "Die Feldgleichungen der Gravitation" - The foundation of the entire simulation.
